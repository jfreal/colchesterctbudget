Summary: Colchester's Spending is Under Control

Over the past 18 fiscal years, Colchester's total budget has grown from $46.9 million to $62.7 million — an increase of 33.5%. During that same period, cumulative inflation (as measured by the Consumer Price Index) rose 51.3%. This means the town's budget has grown at a rate significantly below inflation.

When you factor in population (which has remained essentially flat at around 15,500–16,000 residents), the picture becomes even clearer. Inflation-adjusted spending per resident has actually declined by 13.1% since FY 2007-08. Colchester residents are getting more government services for fewer real dollars per person.

The town's annual budget compound growth rate of just 1.7% reflects disciplined fiscal management — including years with flat or negative budgets (FY 2009-10, FY 2012-13, FY 2020-21). Even the largest single-year increase (5.1% in FY 2024-25) came after years of below-inflation growth and remains modest by any municipal standard.

Compared to its neighbors, Colchester's FY 2024-25 mill rate of 28.67 is the 3rd lowest among 7 surrounding towns and 8% below the neighbor average of 31.1 mills. East Hampton (38.04) and Marlborough (36.29) have significantly higher mill rates.

Among demographic peer towns statewide (similar population, income, and character), Colchester has the lowest per capita spending at $3,979 — 8.2% below the peer average of $4,334. Ellington ($4,295), East Hampton ($4,326), Ledyard ($4,328), and Tolland ($4,390) all spend more per resident. This confirms that Colchester is not just keeping pace — it's leading in fiscal efficiency.
